Why I Do This

Many people, use software as a service without realizing it. They think they own their data until something inconvenient happens, usually the service declines in quality. This is called, platform decay, it is a process in which online products and services decline in quality over time. Initially, vendors create high-quality offerings, attracting users, then they degrade those offerings to better serve business customers, and finally degrade their services to both users and business customers to "maximize short-term profits". As this happens more frequently, I wonder why people continue to use These services. Crave, netflix, spotify, youtube, (anything apple and google produce, honestly) It's not that they're unaware of a better solution. It's not that they aren't capable. I think some people just don't want to do it themselves.

I think people are more capable than many corporations would have you believe. Big tech (google specifically) is selling the general public a lie in that regard. Free, open source software, is safe, it's software you own, and is often more private. That's why I thought it'd be a cool idea to give people the tools and resources they need, to build or find a better solution for their problems, and they can choose to implement it themselves, or hire someone like me to do it for them.
