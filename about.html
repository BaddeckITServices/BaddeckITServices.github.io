Why did I Do This

I'm tired of seeing friends and family become dependent on online services they don't own or control.

People watch videos on YouTube but complain about ads. They subscribe to multiple streaming services and still can't watch everything they want. They rely on AI to write code, YouTube to host their videos, social media and SMS for communication, and companies like Microsoft and Sony to play games or chat online.

All the paid tools suffer the same faults and caveats piracy has. Prime and netflix have ads. Google playstore has borderline malware. Youtube has given me more popups than a torrenting site.

p
I believe the problem today is platform dependence.

When you rely on a platform you don't own, you give that platform power over your data, your access, your workflow, and sometimes even your purchases.

Many "free" services are funded by collecting user data or encouraging users to become paying subscribers. Once enough people depend on a platform, switching away becomes increasingly difficult because of the time, money, and effort already invested.

This creates an opportunity for abuse. Platform owners can change prices, remove features, alter policies, or discontinue products entirely, knowing that many users will accept those changes because the cost of leaving is too high.

This is why I'm interested in open-source software, self-hosting, and technologies that people can actually own and control.

I don't want people to depend on me either.

My goal is to provide open-source IT services where everything I build is freely available. Anyone should be able to inspect it, modify it, and run it themselves. If someone has the time and ability, they shouldn't need me.

The service I provide isn't access to proprietary software. It's my experience, support, installation, customization, maintenance, and troubleshooting.

People can choose to do it themselves, or they can choose to hire me to save time.
