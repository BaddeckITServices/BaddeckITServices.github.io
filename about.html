Why I Do This

I think I'm just tired of all my friends and family relying on services. You watch videos on youtube but complain about ads, you subscribe to all these subscription services and still can't see all the content you want.

People rely on ai for writing code, or rely on youtube to host videos, rely on social media and sms for communication. They use microsoft and playstation to play games and use voice chat online. People are relying on tools and services which they do not own, are not private, and mostly offer these services for free, to collect user data.

This opens them up to abuse. The platform owners can make changes at their whim, and users dependent on the platform will have to live with it, because they're too afraid of the "switching costs".

A good example of companies taking advantage of user dependence, is ai. ChatGPT released free api, and chat bot, then they took away features, then you don't even get api access for free. Now they barely even let you use a decent model for very long before limiting you. They really want you to use that subscription. For people who started coding in the mid 2020s or think they started coding, they rely on it. They need the subscription because they won't do it themselves. In this way they are being taken advantage of finanacially and hurting their brains in the process.

As an example, people play games on xbox and playstation. But xbox live and playstation subscriptions can end up changing. Xbox live used to be free. Then it was broken up into silver and gold (gold gave you 3 free games a month and you could play games online). Then they removed silver, it's just gold. Then they add gamepass, remove gold. Now it's just like 3 different versions of gamepass.

Or a more recent example in 2026. Playstation users who purchased movies, had them removed from their library because of some legal reasons.

These are examples of, platform decay, it is a process in which online products and services decline in quality over time. Initially, vendors create high-quality offerings, attracting users, then they degrade those offerings to better serve business customers, and finally degrade their services to both users and business customers to "maximize short-term profits".

I just want open source IT services. Like what I do is freely available for people to do themselves. You just have the option for paying me to do it.
